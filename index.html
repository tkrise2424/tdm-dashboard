01 — AI Insights

TDM's outreach engine is fully live and generating early traction across LinkedIn and email. 1,269 LinkedIn requests produced 174 accepted connections (13.7%), 146 conversations, and 33 replies. Simultaneously, 3,913 email executions are returning a 3.53% open rate and 3.68% click rate — a healthy cold outreach baseline. The standout is Chris Torbit's ProfServices IT campaign, running at 2.3x the portfolio acceptance rate with the most conversations of any campaign.

📡 Where Engagement Is Strongest

Engagement is concentrated at the LinkedIn acceptance-to-conversation layer. Chris ProfServices (61 conversations) and Diana Mfg IT AI (38.5% reply-to-accepted) show the deepest prospect engagement. Email engagement is strongest at the click layer — 144 clicks vs 138 opens signals prospects are acting without being tracked as "opens," a positive deliverability signal.

🔁 Multi-Touchpoint Engagement Signals

The Conrad Compare web agent has captured an inbound lead (AT&T Consultant, Springfield OH) from the TDM website — a prospect who engaged via web + potential email overlap. Additionally, 440 anonymous website visitors across TDM.cc, clarusco.com, and resolve.clarusco.com represent contacts who have shown intent on the web and may be receiving email simultaneously — a high-value cross-channel segment not yet activated.

⚡ Rapid Engagement Patterns

Diana's Mfg IT AI campaign shows disproportionate reply velocity relative to acceptance volume — 5 replies from 19 accepted contacts (38.5%) indicates prospects are responding quickly after connecting, not going cold. This is a strong signal of message-market fit and audience readiness. Compare to the portfolio average of 19.0% reply-to-accepted.

🎯 Funnel Concentration Risk

The majority of conversations (61 of 146, 42%) are coming from a single campaign — Chris ProfServices. While this validates the campaign, it also means overall conversation volume is highly dependent on one rep and one audience. Replicating this campaign's connection note and audience logic across Ed and Robert's campaigns is the highest-leverage action available.

05 — Performance Insights
  • 🏆
    High acceptance rate confirms audience-message fit — Chris ProfServices is the benchmark. 236 requests → 75 accepted (31.8%) → 61 conversations → 20 replies. A 31.8% acceptance rate is 2.3x the portfolio average (13.7%), which is a direct signal that the connection note language and audience targeting are aligned. This campaign's connection message and ICP definition should be extracted and used as the template for Ed and Robert's next campaign builds.
    High acceptance rate (31.8%) → confirms audience-message fit → replicate connection note across other reps
  • 💎
    High reply-to-accepted rate signals strong message resonance — Diana Mfg IT AI is the quality leader. 5 replies from 19 accepted contacts (38.5% reply-to-accepted) is the highest quality engagement ratio in the portfolio. Prospects are responding quickly after connecting, not going cold. The constraint is acceptance volume (only 19 from 165 requests). Improving the connection request note for this audience is the single highest-leverage action available for Diana's campaigns.
    High reply-to-accepted rate (38.5%) → strong post-connect message resonance → increase volume + optimize connection note
  • 📧
    Clicks outpacing opens indicates CTA effectiveness despite tracking limitations. 144 clicks vs 138 opens is unusual and positive — it means prospects are clicking through without being tracked as "opens," common when email clients block tracking pixels (preview panes, Apple Mail Privacy Protection). The content and subject lines are working. The gap to fix is not engagement — it's the absence of a reply-ask CTA that converts clickers into responders.
    Clicks (144) > Opens (138) → tracking pixel suppression, not disengagement → content is working, optimize for replies not clicks
  • ⚠️
    Zero email replies across 3,913 sends indicates CTA friction, not interest gap. Prospects are opening and clicking but not replying. This is a CTA design problem — current sequences drive traffic to external links (landing pages, press releases) rather than prompting a direct reply. The interest signal is present (3.68% click rate). The friction is in the ask. Adding a soft reply-ask ("Does this apply to your situation? Just hit reply.") to step 2 will test whether the interest converts to conversation.
    0 email replies despite 144 clicks → CTA sends to links, not reply prompts → add reply-ask to step 2 of top sequences
  • 🔴
    Low acceptance rate signals audience-message mismatch — Diana Mfg IT Demand Gen needs a reset. 3.6% acceptance on 165 requests (6 accepted, 0 replies) is the weakest result in the portfolio and is 3x worse than Diana's AI campaign targeting the same vertical (11.5%). The difference is likely the connection note — the AI campaign's message is outperforming, which means the Demand Gen note is the variable to fix. Pausing volume and A/B testing a new connection note is the right next step.
    Low acceptance rate (3.6%) → audience-message mismatch on connection note → pause, rewrite, A/B test before resuming
  • 🌐
    Website visitor lists represent the highest-intent untapped segment in the funnel. 440 anonymous visitors across TDM.cc (199), clarusco.com (170), and resolve.clarusco.com (71) have already demonstrated intent by visiting the site. These contacts sit above cold outreach in the funnel — they've self-selected into awareness. No outreach sequences are currently targeting them. Activating this segment is the fastest path to higher-quality conversations.
    440 warm website visitors with no active outreach → highest-intent untapped segment → build dedicated sequences, expected higher response rate vs. cold
